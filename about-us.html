About Us

Purpose:

As indigenous professionals on humanitarian sectors and capacity buildings, they felt that there is an argent need to interventions and they had the ability to access the grass roots and act as a bridge between the suffering and desperate local communities and the UN agencies, International organizations, well-wishers and charities that are willing to help the Somali people. This was the philosophy behind the establishment of TTAF Somalia.

Vision:

TTAF is committed to working in partnership with communities to address humanitarian needs, improve livelihoods, and to support overall peaceful, healthy and sustainable development.

TAF offers a cultural group to perform in any national or international venue and, cultural dance or drumming training for interested individuals or groups and also gender balance, We believe it is important to maintain our environment clean and to manage well our waste. Thus, in Somalia we offer Waste management and cleaning and beautification services.

Mission

TTAF works to reduce poverty and human suffering in emergency and disaster situations and contribute to an overall improvement in the Standard of living and quality of life for communities in Somalia.

Organizational Objectives:
  • To ensure sustainable food security and income generation through improved crop production, animal husbandry, infrastructure rehabilitation, cash-based livelihood interventions, improved markets and market chain linking
  • To enhance education and skill training opportunities for youth and adults and to help to strengthen educational systems and livelihood Standards
  • To improve access to safe water, sanitation, and hygiene facilities and facilitate acceptable Standards of humanitarian services for displaced persons and refugees
  • To advocate for responsible utilization and management of natural resources
  • To advocate for good governance and human rights
  • To carry out surveys and studies that will promote strategic planning and informed decision making.
Core Values:

Below are the core values that drive TTAF:

  1. Credibility: The value of any inquiry lies in the usefulness of its findings and the appropriateness of the methodology used in the inquiry. TTAF seeks to generate findings that are of immediate use to the communities in their quest for sustainable integrated development. Our services are thus the result of credible processes that are easily verifiable;
  2. Equality: The essence of communities lies in their capacity to weave their diversities into a common strength against adversities. The Somali Society is characterized of Gender and social disparities, which are factors in any developmental efforts. In order to give all sectors of the Society within our areas of operation an opportunity ‘TTAF applies at all times the principle of equality. This is ensured by basing all our interventions on the scale and levels of the need and not on the identity or characteristics of the needy;
  3. Respect: The primary – only claimants of the services provided by ‘TTAF are people - human beings. Their being human exerts from us the responsibility to acknowledge them as such and treat them with respect. In this regard, all interactions by ‘TTAF are characterized by mutual respect – respect of ourselves and our Institution and for our clients;
  4. Integrity: ‘TTAF is committed to uphold the highest moral TTAF ndards in our operations and in this respect interacts with others honestly and faithfully;
  5. Dignity: ‘TTAF holds every person to be worthy of esteem and respect. We consciously uphold and promote the dignity of all our clients in all our dealings.
